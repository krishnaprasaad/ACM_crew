All that you need to know about CyberX

  • CyberX is an innovative hackathon organized by VIT Chennai in association with the Greater Chennai Police in order to foster development of tools and technology.
  • The domain for the hackathon is CYBER SECURITY.
  • The hackathon will be held in a hybrid form with the intial idea submission round being online and the grand finale at VIT, Chennai campus.
  • The hackathon aims to bring together the brightest minds and and focus their efforts towards building the tech of tomorrow and to help serve society.

Prizes:
  • Register today for winning exciting cash prizes. l Prize - Rs. 50,000, II prize - Rs.30 000 and III prize - Rs. 20,000.
Grand finale participants get an additional bonus as well. They are eligible for free training from the security industry Alibi technology in the field of mobile and hard disc forensics worth ₹45000.
  • Register in a team of 1-3 members and get through the idea submission round ending 02.05.2023 to earn a spot in the grand finale held in person at VIT Chennai. In addition, all applicants are eligible for participation certificates

Guidelines:

  • The hackathon will have two stages. Registration wil be open till 30.04.2023 (23:59 IST). Registration is open to all Indian citizens and is free of charge.
  • The initial idea submission (Phase 1) round will accept submissions during the window 11.04.2023, 08:00 IST to 02.05.2023, 23:59 IST. Phase 1 results will be announced on 10.05.2023.
  • The grand finale (for shortlisted participants) will be held on 19.05.2023 and 20.05.2023 at VIT Chennai Campus.
  • Details regarding the finale will be intimated to shortlisted participants over mail.

Rules:
Respect:
  • Participants should respect each other, the judges, mentors, and organizers.
  • Any form of discrimination, harassment, or bullying will not be tolerated.

 Original Work:
  • Participants must create original work during the hackathon.
  • Copying or plagiarism is not allowed and may result in disqualification.

 Use of Resources:
  • Participants can use open source code and libraries but should not use code or materials that they do not have the right to use or distribute.

 Code Quality:
  • All code must be well-documented and maintainable.
  • Participants should avoid hacks and shortcuts that could compromise the quality and security of their project. 

Teamwork:
  • Teams should work collaboratively and fairly.
  • Any attempt to sabotage or disrupt another team's work will not be tolerated.

Presentation:
  • Teams must present their project to the judges within the given time limit.
  • Presentations should be clear, concise, and engaging.

Safety:
  • Participants must follow safety guidelines when using hardware, software, or any other equipment during the hackathon.

Code Ownership:
  • Participants own the intellectual property rights to their projects, but they must also make their code open source and available to others.

Time Management:
  • Participants should manage their time effectively to ensure that they complete their project within the given time limit.

Feedback:
  • Participants should be open to feedback and constructive criticism from judges. 

Final Authority:
  • Decisions made by judges and the organizing committee will be at their discretion and will be treated as final.

Idea Submission Round (Phase 1)
  • In this round, we expect you to submit two things - a PowerPoint presentation and a pitch video.
  • The PowerPoint presentation should include your problem. statement, your solution, and the impact and USP of your solution.
  • Be terse in the ppt, and save the details for the pitch video.
  • The video is expected to be 1-3 minutes long and should explain your product in detail.
  • Please ensure that both the ppt and the video are uploaded as publicly available before you share the links with us.

About Greater Chennai Police:

    Chennai city Police has traversed 167 years on the sands of time, leaving many a distinct footprint behind which dates back to 1629 A.D. The City Police has undergone a sea change from its initial days. Greater Chennai Police has served as model in urban policing, starting from turn out and touching ideal standards of response time, detection community service and maintenance of Law and Order. Greater Chennai Police with a long and illustrious history has established itself as a truly professional and trend setting Police Force. In line with professionalism, Greater Chennai Police is conducted this Cyber Hackathon join hands with VIT to find out solution for the real time problems that Police Force facing every day.

About Alibi:
  • Alibi is India's first NABL Accredited Cyber Forensic Private Lab, based in Kerala and with Operational Centres in Tamil Nadu and Delhi, and specialising solely in cyber forensics and cyber intelligence. Alibi is a certified ISO/IEC 17001:2017 and ISO/IEC 27001:2013 company.

Training Program:
  • Alibi offers to provide free training in the field of mobile and hard disc forensics to the top 5 teams in the CyberX Hackathon, which costs about Rs.45,000 per person. The training and certification will be immensely beneficial to the trainees' careers. The training period is 40 hours, and certification will be provided at the completion of the training.

Benefits:
  • The trainee will be exposed to actual forensic procedural approach for dealing with any crime involving digital evidence. The trainee will also have the chance to work on actual cases and gain hands-on experience with globally renowned licensed Cyber forensic software tools.

Duration and Schedule Details:
  • The training is available both online and offline formats. Offline training is only available at the Chennai office of Alibi.
Training will take place between the 5th June and 8th July, 2023. The training will take place from Monday to Friday, for a total of 40 hours and the trainee could choose the date of their convenience.
